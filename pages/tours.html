Cultural Treasures

This package is perfect for travelers who are interested in history and culture. It takes you on a journey through Sri Lanka's ancient past, visiting impressive sites like Sigiriya, Polonnaruwa, and the Temple of the Tooth in Kandy. You'll be amazed by the intricate carvings, majestic architecture, and the stories behind each of these incredible cultural treasures.

Complete Tour Plan

1: Arrival in Colombo

Upon your arrival at the Bandaranaike International Airport in Colombo, our tour representative will meet you and transfer you to your hotel. Spend the rest of the day at leisure, enjoying the hotel's amenities or exploring the city

Day 2: Sigiriya and Dambulla

After breakfast, we'll depart for Sigiriya, home to the ancient rock fortress of Sigiriya. Climb the impressive rock fortress and marvel at the breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. Afterward, we'll visit the nearby Dambulla Cave Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that features a series of cave temples filled with ancient murals and statues

Day 3: Polonnaruwa and Minneriya National Park

Today, we'll visit the ancient city of Polonnaruwa, another UNESCO World Heritage Site that was once the capital of Sri Lanka. Explore the ruins of ancient palaces, temples, and statues that date back to the 12th century. In the afternoon, we'll take a jeep safari in Minneriya National Park, home to herds of elephants and other wildlife

Day 4: Kandy

After breakfast, we'll depart for Kandy, the cultural capital of Sri Lanka. On the way, we'll stop at a spice garden to learn about the island's famous spices. In the evening, we'll visit the Temple of the Tooth, one of the most sacred Buddhist temples in Sri Lanka, which houses a relic of the Buddha's tooth

Day 5: Kandy City Tour

Today, we'll take a city tour of Kandy, visiting the Royal Botanical Gardens, the Kandy Lake, and the bustling Kandy market. In the evening, we'll enjoy a traditional Kandyan dance performance

Day 6: Colombo City Tour

After breakfast, we'll depart for Colombo, Sri Lanka's commercial capital. We'll take a city tour of Colombo, visiting the Gangaramaya Temple, the National Museum, and the Independence Square. In the evening, we'll have dinner at a local restaurant before returning to our hotel

Day 7: Departure from Colombo

After breakfast, we'll transfer you to the airport for your departure flight. You'll leave Sri Lanka with unforgettable memories of the country's rich cultural heritage and natural beauty

Attractions

Arrival in Colombo

After your arrival at the Bandaranaike International Airport in Colombo, you'll be transferred to your hotel in Negombo, a coastal town located just a short distance from the airport. Negombo is known for its long stretch of beach, bustling fish market, and colonial-era architecture. You can spend the afternoon relaxing on the beach or exploring the town's sights.

Bentota Beach

Bentota is a popular beach destination located on the southwest coast of Sri Lanka, known for its golden sand beaches and calm waters. Here, you can enjoy various water sports like surfing, jet skiing, and snorkeling. You can also take a boat ride through the mangrove-lined lagoon or visit the nearby Turtle Hatchery, which aims to conserve and protect endangered sea turtles.

Unawatuna Beach

Unawatuna is a charming coastal town located on the southern coast of Sri Lanka, known for its pristine beaches and rich cultural heritage. The nearby historic Galle Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a must-visit attraction. Built by the Portuguese in the 16th century and later expanded by the Dutch, the fort is a remarkable testament to Sri Lanka's colonial past. You can also visit the nearby Rumassala Hill, a forested nature reserve that offers panoramic views of the coastline.

Mirissa Beach

Mirissa is a laid-back beach town located on the southern coast of Sri Lanka, known for its picturesque beach and excellent whale watching opportunities. You can take a boat tour to see blue whales, sperm whales, and dolphins up close in their natural habitat. You can also explore the nearby fishing village and sample fresh seafood dishes.

Complete Tour Plan

Day 1: Arrival in Colombo

You'll arrive in Colombo, where our friendly driver will meet you at the airport and transport you to your hotel in Negombo. After checking in and getting some rest, you can explore the charming coastal town on your own or take a leisurely stroll along the beach.

Day 2: Wilpattu National Park

After breakfast, we'll depart for Wilpattu National Park, the largest wildlife reserve in Sri Lanka. You'll embark on an exciting jeep safari through the dense jungle, where you'll encounter a variety of exotic animals including leopards, elephants, sloth bears, and spotted deer. After a thrilling day in the park, we'll retire to our comfortable accommodation nearby.

Day 3: Anuradhapura

We'll start the day with a visit to the ancient city of Anuradhapura, which was the capital of Sri Lanka for over 1,300 years. You'll explore the ruins of the city, including the majestic Ruwanwelisaya stupa, the Isurumuniya rock temple, and the magnificent Abhayagiriya monastery.

Day 4: Minneriya National Park

Our next stop is the Minneriya National Park, which is famous for its large herds of wild elephants. You'll embark on another thrilling jeep safari through the park, where you'll get to see these majestic creatures up close and personal. You may also encounter other wildlife such as sambar deer, macaques, and crocodiles.

Day 5: Sigiriya and Polonnaruwa

Today we'll visit two UNESCO World Heritage sites - the ancient rock fortress of Sigiriya and the medieval city of Polonnaruwa. You'll climb the steep stairs to the top of Sigiriya, where you'll witness breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. In Polonnaruwa, you'll explore the well-preserved ruins of the ancient city, including the impressive Parakrama Samudra reservoir.

Day 6: Yala National Park

We'll depart for Yala National Park, which is home to the highest concentration of leopards in the world. You'll embark on another exciting jeep safari through the park, where you'll encounter a variety of wildlife including elephants, sloth bears, water buffalo, and colorful birds. After a long day of adventure, we'll retire to our comfortable accommodation near the park.

Day 7: Departure

On the final day of our tour, we'll transport you back to Colombo for your departure flight. You'll leave with unforgettable memories of the stunning wildlife and rich culture of Sri Lanka.

Hill Country Escape

This package is perfect for travelers who want to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and experience the natural beauty of Sri Lanka's hill country. You'll visit picturesque towns like Nuwara Eliya and Ella, explore tea plantations, and hike through misty mountains. You'll be amazed by the breathtaking scenery and fresh mountain air.

Complete Tour Plan

Day 1: Kandy

You will start your tour by exploring the ancient city of Kandy, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visit the sacred Temple of the Tooth, which houses the relic of Buddha's tooth, and take a stroll around the serene Kandy Lake. You can also witness the traditional Kandyan dance performance in the evening.

Day 2-3: Nuwara Eliya

On the second day, you will drive through the winding roads and picturesque landscapes to reach Nuwara Eliya, the heart of Sri Lanka's tea country. You will explore the lush tea plantations, visit a tea factory, and taste some of the finest Ceylon tea. You can also visit the Horton Plains National Park, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to a unique ecosystem of cloud forests and grasslands. The park is famous for its stunning natural attractions such as Baker's Falls and World's End.

Day 4-5: Ella

Next, you will drive to Ella, a small charming town in the highlands of Sri Lanka. Ella is famous for its stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys, picturesque tea plantations, and cascading waterfalls. You can hike to the top of Little Adam's Peak for panoramic views, visit the Nine Arch Bridge, and explore the Rawana Ella Cave Temple.

Day 6-7: Tissamaharama

Finally, you will drive down to Tissamaharama, which is the gateway to Sri Lanka's southern wildlife reserves. You will visit the Yala National Park, which is home to a wide range of animals, including elephants, leopards, sloth bears, and crocodiles. You can also take a boat safari on the serene Tissa Lake to witness the stunning birdlife and water creatures.
